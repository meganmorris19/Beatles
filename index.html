The Beatles were some of the most influential musicians in the history of popular music — and they've just decided to digitally stream their music.


Want to learn more about the band? Scroll down.


The four Beatles were John Lennon, Paul McCartney, George Harrison, and Ringo Starr. Scroll right to learn more about each member and how they contributed to one of the greatest bands the world has ever seen.



Lennon was the singer-songwriter who founded the Beatles. Lennon was living in Liverpool in 1957 when he met Paul McCartney, decided to partner with him, and began to change the music world.



McCartney played bass for the Beatles, and he has either written or co-written 32 songs that have reached the #1 spot on Billboard’s Hot 100 list. After the band’s break-up, McCartney formed a band called Wings, and later began a solo career.



Harrison was brought onto the band in 1958 by Paul McCartney, and he became the lead guitarist of the Beatles. Harrison wrote several of the band’s songs, including (perhaps most famously) “Something.”


Starr — born Richard Starkey — was the drummer for the Beatles. He became famous by drumming for the band Rory Storm and the Hurricanes, and he joined the Beatles in 1962 (replacing their original drummer, Pete Best).


On December 24, 2015, at 12:01 a.m., the Beatles released their music for the first time on digital streaming platforms such as Spotify, Google Play, Tidal and Amazon Prime. The British band — considered one of the most influential bands in the history of music — has had its songs played millions of times since the release.


The Beatles’ decision to digitially stream their music so many years after digital streaming’s beginning fits the band’s pattern of allowing technological developments in the music world to prove their worth before taking advantage of them.
